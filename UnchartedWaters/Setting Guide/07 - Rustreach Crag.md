# [Rustreach Crag](#rustreach-crag)

A steep, jagged rock, called the Fang, riddled with vertical mine shafts plunging into the dark. The settlement clings to the outside in a lattice of industrial balconies and cranes, all devoted to dragging ore up from the depths.

- **Vibe:** Industrial, vertical, metallic. Sulfur and wet stone, rust and copper oxidation on everything.
- **Hub:** **The Winch-Works**, an open-air platform halfway up the Fang: smelting furnaces, the Overseer's office, and the *Bucket & Tankard* tavern.
- **Hook:** The deeper the shafts go, the more the rock bleeds warm saltwater, flooding tunnels faster than they can be reinforced. Rumors claim the lowest levels have breached a cavern of **Living Copper**, pulsing like a heart and drawing strange metallic-shelled creatures up from the depths.
- **Occupants:** Shaft Miners, Flood-Runners (racing rising floodwater underground), plus the two resident factions below.
- **Facilities:** Primary Lift Shafts, Smelt Platforms, Pump Galleries, The Abyssal Cradle (Deep Crawlers').

## Faction: The Copper Coalition

- **Who:** An industrial powerhouse on volatile volcanic vents, driven by a brutal work ethic to produce the strongest alloys found anywhere on the sea.
- **Leader:** Overseer Bram, cares more about quotas than safety.
- **Ship:** The Copper Ember.
- **Presence here:** Owns the vertical mine shafts. Industrial balconies and steam-powered cranes dominate the cliff face.
- **Motivation:** Provide the metal and fuel every other faction needs to build ships and weapons.

## Faction: The Deep Crawlers

- **Who:** Deep sea scavengers who treat the ocean floor as an unplundered museum. The primary source of Old World technology and metals.
- **Leader:** Foreman Grogori, spends more time in a brass diving suit than out of it.
- **Ship:** The Azure Abyss.
- **Presence here:** Controls the Abyssal Cradle at the Crag's submerged base, using the steep cliffs as a vertical highway into the dark.
- **Motivation:** Recover Old World technology and artifacts before they're lost to the silt forever.
