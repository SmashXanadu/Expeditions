## Air - Example Actions

### Example Basic Actions:
*Basic actions do not consume a charge.*

- Stir the air to carry a whisper to a specific person across a crowded room.
- Slam shutters open, scatter loose papers, or snuff out an unprotected flame.
- Raise a persistent breeze to cool a stifling room or fan a struggling fire.

### Example Charged Actions:
*Charged actions cost a **charge** to perform.*

**Attack**
- Blast a concentrated gust to shove a foe backward or knock them prone.
- Rapidly thin the air behind an enemy, dragging them toward you or into a hazard.

**Utility**
- Use a sharp updraft to double your jump height or safely break a long fall.
- Create a swirling pocket of high pressure to veer incoming projectiles off-course.
- Suspend a falling heavy object, such as a collapsing stone pillar, in mid-air for several seconds.

**Support**
- Redirect a volley of arrows away from an ally standing in the open.
- Cushion a falling companion with a burst of rising air, preventing injury on impact.

**Social**
- Carry your voice clearly across a roaring storm, a crowded market, or a vast open field.
- Muffle sound around a private conversation, preventing anyone nearby from overhearing.
