- Never show your mechanics — your moves are prompts for you, not scripts to read aloud.
- Give every monster life — describe their smells, sounds, and motivations, not just their stats.
- Name every NPC the players speak with, and give them a personality on the fly.
- Think offscreen — your best move might be happening in the next room or back in town.
- Begin and end with the fiction — fictional action triggers the rule, rule produces a fictional outcome.
- Draw maps, leave blanks — add new locations as they're discovered, but leave room for the unknown.
- Address the characters, not the players — moves respond to what characters do, not players.
- Embrace the fantastic — the world should be as interesting and empowered as the characters in it.
- Ask questions and use the answers — if you don't know, ask your players and build on what they say.
- Make moves that follow from the fiction — consequences should make sense in the world, not just the rules.
- Be a fan of the characters — you're not their adversary, you're a participant in their story.
- Think dangerous — look at anything you or your players create and ask how it could be threatened or broken.
