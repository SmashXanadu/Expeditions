## [Giant Rat](#giant-rat)

**Type:** Beast | **Size:** Medium

| HP | DPR | Atk | Mag | Def | Res |
|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|
| 3  | 2   | 10  | 8   | 10  | 8   |

| Abilities | Effect |
|:--|:--|
| **Gnaw** | A savage bite that tears and holds. |
| **Disease Bite** | TN10 or infected — take 1 damage at the start of each combat until treated. |
| **Swarm Frenzy** | If two or more Giant Rats are adjacent to the same target, they each gain +2 Atk. |

**Description:**
- Dog-sized rat with matted greasy fur, yellowed incisors as long as daggers, red-rimmed frantic eyes
- Moves in quick unpredictable bursts — never a straight line, always looking for an angle
- Found in sewers, dungeon cellars, abandoned granaries, and the holds of neglected ships

**Tactics:**
- Never attacks alone if it can help it — waits for a packmate to engage first to trigger Swarm Frenzy
- Uses Disease Bite on downed or prone targets
- Flees through the smallest available gap when reduced to 1 HP
- Swarms any warmth source; will brave fire for food

| Consequences | Minor | Major |
|:--|:--|:--|
| **Attacking** | It bites back — take 1 damage and make a Disease Bite check<br>Drop your weapon<br>Disadvantage on next attack | Infected wound that won't close. Permanent disadvantage on attack rolls until treated. |
| **Defending** | Take 1 damage<br>Lose a food item or consumable to gnawing<br>Disadvantage on next action | Bitten deep. Permanently lose 1 HP from max. |
| **Sneaking** | You disturb a nest. Drop an item<br>Take 1 damage<br>Disadvantage on next stealth roll | Scent-marked by the colony. Permanent stealth disadvantage in sewers and dungeons. |
| **Fleeing** | Drop an item (they'll take it)<br>Take 1 damage<br>Disadvantage on next action | Chased into the heart of the colony. Permanently lose 1 HP from max. |
| **Interacting** | Lose food or a consumable<br>Take 1 damage from a test bite<br>Disadvantage on next social roll | Marked as prey. Rats in this area always attack you on sight. |

| Loot (1d20) | Result |
|:--:|:--|
| 1–8 | Nothing useful — but the nest may contain chewed coins or lost trinkets |
| 9–14 | Intact disease gland (alchemical component for poisons or antidotes), large claws |
| 15–18 | A stolen item from a previous victim, still in good condition |
| 19–20 | The rats were guarding something they don't understand / a ring swallowed long ago bearing a noble crest |
