## [Bandit Captain](#bandit-captain)

**Type:** Humanoid | **Size:** Medium

| HP | DPR | Atk | Mag | Def | Res |
|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|
| 4  | 2   | 12  | 12  | 16  | 13  |

| Abilities | Effect |
|:--|:--|
| **Commanding Strike** | Attacks and simultaneously directs one ally to attack the same target as a free action. |
| **Disarm** | TN12 or the target drops their weapon. |
| **Rally** | Once per combat, all allied Bandits within range recover 1 HP and may immediately act. |

**Description:**
- Better armor than the rest, a trophy or two from past victories, calm and assessing eyes
- Stands slightly apart from the group, watching — steps in only when the outcome is in question
- Found commanding bandit camps, fortified strongholds, or aboard pirate vessels

**Tactics:**
- Lets bandits absorb the first hits while reading the party's strengths
- Targets the best fighter with Disarm; uses Commanding Strike to pile on afterward
- Saves Rally for when the tide turns — times it to prevent a route, not to win outright
- Will negotiate if clearly losing — always has something to offer or a secret to leverage

| Consequences | Minor | Major |
|:--|:--|:--|
| **Attacking** | Counter-riposte — take 1 damage<br>Drop your weapon<br>Disadvantage on next attack | Precise blade work leaves a permanent scar. Permanent disadvantage on attack rolls. |
| **Defending** | Take 1 damage<br>Lose a piece of gear in a tactical strip<br>Disadvantage on next action | Made an example of in front of the crew. Permanently lose 1 HP from max. |
| **Sneaking** | The Captain always keeps a watch. Drop an item<br>Take 1 damage<br>Disadvantage on next stealth roll | Your face is now known to the network. Permanent stealth disadvantage near this gang. |
| **Fleeing** | Drop an item<br>Take 1 damage<br>Disadvantage on next action | The Captain always collects debts. Permanently lose 1 HP from max. |
| **Interacting** | Lose a significant trade item<br>Take 1 damage<br>Disadvantage on next social roll | Outplayed in negotiation. Permanently hostile reputation with this captain's network. |

| Loot (1d20) | Result |
|:--:|:--|
| 1–8 | Coin purse (4d6 gold), quality road gear, a spare weapon |
| 9–14 | A contract listing a patron's name, a command seal or badge of authority |
| 15–18 | A quality enchanted weapon or piece of armor, letters of marque |
| 19–20 | Evidence of a conspiracy reaching far higher than bandits / a treasure map with a significant cache marked |
