## The Troll Bridge

**Difficulty:** 3 Star     |     **Duration:** 1 Hour

> "By order of the Stoneford Town Council:
>
> The Millrace Bridge crossing has been closed for eleven days following a series of fatal incidents involving a large hostile creature residing beneath the eastern arch.
>
> The Council is offering a bounty of twenty-five gold coins for proof of the creature's permanent removal. The crossing generates revenue for this town that we cannot afford to lose.
>
> Any party accepting this contract assumes full liability for themselves and their equipment.
>
> The bridge is a two-hour walk east on the trade road. You cannot miss the smell."
>
> — *Alderman Cobb, Stoneford Town Council*

\newpage

## The Troll Bridge
### Alderman Cobb

- **Description:** A thin, fussy man with ink-stained fingers and a wig slightly too large for his head. Carries a ledger he keeps referring to even during conversations that have nothing to do with numbers.
- **Goal:** Reopen the bridge before the quarterly trade tally — the lost revenue is already noted in the ledger and it is bothering him enormously.
- **Voice:** Formal and slightly condescending. Softens immediately when nervous. Uses the phrase "as a matter of record" far too often.

**The Journey:** East along the trade road...

- Three abandoned wagons on the roadside — goods still loaded, horses long gone. One has a dried bloodstain on the driver's bench.
- A tollkeeper's hut at the road's edge, door hanging open. A half-eaten meal still on the table inside.
- The gorge becomes audible before the bridge is visible — rushing water below, and a low rhythmic sound that might be snoring.

### The Location: Millrace Bridge

- **Outside:** A wide stone arch bridge spanning a deep river gorge. Old and solid — dwarven-built, older than the town. The center of the bridge is stained dark. A makeshift "CLOSED" sign hangs from the railing, ignored by the troll.
- **Under:** A cavernous hollow beneath the eastern arch, accessible from the rocky riverbank. Low ceiling, packed dirt floor, walls piled with coins, bones, broken wagon wheels, and crushed luggage.
- **Atmosphere:** The roar of the river below fills everything. Wet stone and something rank — old meat and river mud. The underside of the bridge is scratched with claw marks reaching fifteen feet up.

### The Situation: The Troll

1 **Troll** (Large) has claimed the bridge as its toll point for three months. It is not clever, but it is patient.

- **Health:** 8
- **Damage:** 3 / round
- **Regenerates** 1 HP at the start of each turn. Fire or acid damage taken since its last turn stops this.
- **Lurks** in the water or beneath the arch; launches onto the bridge the moment targets reach the center.
- **Ignores** smaller wounds entirely and walks through missile fire without flinching.
- **Retreats** into the river if set on fire — submerges to extinguish, resurfaces angrier on the far bank.

**Possible Discoveries**

• **The Hoard** — Eleven weeks of collected "tolls." Roughly 40 gold in mixed coin, plus jewelry and a quality short sword still in its scabbard.
• **The Wanted Poster** — Rolled and tucked in a crack in the arch stone: a missing persons notice for one Garrett Finch, merchant, last seen on the trade road. The troll ate someone who was being looked for.
• **The Scorch Mark** — One bridge pillar is blackened from an old fire — someone tried torching it before. It clearly worked on some level; the troll avoids that pillar.
• **The River Exit** — A narrow ledge below the arch follows the riverbank east to a shallow crossing point. The troll knows about it. So can the party.

**The Clock** : 1 Hour
A 12-wagon merchant caravan is one hour out on the trade road. If the troll isn't dealt with, the party watches it roll straight into an ambush. The Council will not pay the bounty if there are civilian casualties.

**Escalations**

• **Into the Water** — The troll dives into the gorge and attacks from the river, making melee difficult. Ranged attacks suffer disadvantage from the spray.
• **The Grab** — The troll seizes a party member and attempts to drag them off the bridge toward the water.
• **Railing Collapse** — A section of bridge railing cracks under the troll's weight and gives way. Anyone near it makes a TN12 check or goes over the edge.

**Rewards**: 25 Gold (council bounty) • Troll's hoard contents • Permanent bridge toll waiver (minor but useful in a continuing campaign)
