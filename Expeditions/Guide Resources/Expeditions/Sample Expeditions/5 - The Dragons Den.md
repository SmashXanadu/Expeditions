## The Dragon's Den

**Difficulty:** 5 Star     |     **Duration:** 1 Hour

> "We have tried tribute. We have tried negotiation. We have tried ignoring it and hoping it would leave.
>
> The creature calling itself **Ashveil** has taken our livestock, our savings, and last week, our miller's son who brought the payment late.
>
> Three villages are pooling everything we have left. It is one hundred gold. We know it is not enough for this kind of work. We are asking anyway.
>
> The fortress it has claimed is called Grimholt Keep. It is half a day's walk into the mountain pass. You will know you are close when the trees stop having leaves.
>
> We cannot survive another tribute cycle."
>
> — *Elder Sera of Thornwall, speaking for Thornwall, Dustmill, and Crestfen*

## The Dragon's Den
### Elder Sera of Thornwall

- **Description:** A tall woman, late sixties, with white hair braided tight and the deliberate stillness of someone who has made hard decisions before. Her hands don't shake when she speaks about the dragon.
- **Goal:** End the tribute permanently — not just this cycle. She wants proof of death, or proof it has left the region entirely.
- **Voice:** Unhurried. Says exactly what she means. Will not be talked into lowering the reward or adding conditions.

**The Journey:** Up the mountain pass road...

- Scorched trees begin a mile out — not wildfire patterns, deliberate sweeps. The dragon has been marking its territory.
- Bleached bones of livestock on both sides of the road, picked clean and arranged. This is a message, not just feeding.
- Grimholt Keep visible ahead: a dwarven fortress carved into the mountainside, partially collapsed, one tower still standing. Distant shapes move in the gatehouse — small, watching.

### The Location: Grimholt Keep

- **Outside:** A collapsed dwarven fortress with three entry points: the main gate (guarded by kobolds), a crumbled section of outer wall (unguarded, unstable), and a hidden mining shaft entrance on the eastern face (narrow, requires searching to find — TN12).
- **Inside:** The great hall still stands, blackened by years of fire and ash. The old throne room vault at the rear has been converted into a hoard chamber. Kobold warrens fill the side passages, with crude shrines to the dragon scratched into the walls.
- **Atmosphere:** Sulfur thickens the air the deeper you go. Every surface coated in old ash. The keep is warm — not from fire currently burning, but from something large that generates its own heat. Absolute silence from the kobolds when the dragon moves.

### The Situation: Ashveil

**Outer Keep — 4 Kobold Guards** (reskinned Goblin Scouts) watch the main gate.

- **Health:** 1
- **Damage:** 1 / round
- **Flee** immediately to warn Ashveil if they spot intruders — they will not fight alone.
- **One kobold, Pip**, can be spotted hanging back from the others, clearly miserable. A kind word and TN10 Interact will convince him to guide the party through the mining shaft entrance instead.

**Great Hall — Ashveil, Young Dragon**

- **Health:** 9
- **Damage:** 4 / round
- **Attack:** 17 | **Magic:** 16 | **Defense:** 16 | **Resistance:** 14
- **Breath** — TN16 or take 4 fire damage. Ashveil uses this at the start of combat and whenever three or more targets are grouped.
- **Takes Flight** when reduced to 5 HP or below — gains +2 Def while airborne. Breath weapon range doubles.
- **Negotiates** when reduced to 3 HP — offers to vacate the region and leave the hoard in exchange for its life. This offer is genuine. Whether to trust it is the party's decision.

Ashveil knows the party is coming. The kobolds sent word the moment strangers entered the pass. It will not be surprised.

**Possible Discoveries**

• **Tribute Records** — A flat stone tablet scratched with a tally of every village payment, what was taken, and dates. Ashveil has been organized and deliberate from the start — this was never random predation.
• **The Eggs** — Two dragon eggs in the vault, warm and clearly viable. Destroying them ends any future threat. Leaving them is a gamble. Taking one is an enormous commitment the party should not make lightly.
• **The Dwarven Map** — An intact wall-engraving in the vault showing the full original fortress layout, including a lower level that Ashveil's fire-damage has sealed off. Whatever is down there, the dragon doesn't know about it.
• **Pip the Kobold** — If treated well throughout, Pip will share that Ashveil arrived six months ago fleeing something — not conquering. He does not know what it was fleeing from.

**The Clock** : 1 Hour
Ashveil's kobold informants sent word when the party entered the pass. If the party has not engaged within one hour of arrival, Ashveil flies to Thornwall preemptively and burns it. Elder Sera will not survive the night.

**Escalations**

• **Airborne** — Ashveil takes to the ceiling of the great hall or exits through the collapsed roof section. Melee becomes nearly impossible; ranged attacks at disadvantage until it lands or is forced down.
• **Ceiling Collapse** — Sustained fire damage weakens the ancient stonework. A section of ceiling drops, creating difficult terrain and separating the party.
• **Kobold Swarm** — Remaining kobolds pour from side passages. They are not a serious threat alone, but they complicate everything else.

**Rewards**: 100 Gold (village contribution) • Ashveil's hoard (negotiated share or full claim) • One dragon scale (rare crafting material, structural quality) • The eggs (player's choice — the implications extend far beyond this expedition)
