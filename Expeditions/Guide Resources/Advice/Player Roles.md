## [Player Roles](#player-roles)

Giving each player a table responsibility keeps everyone engaged between their turns and reduces the load on the Guide. Assign one role per player at the start of the campaign. Roles can rotate between sessions if the group prefers.

| Role | Responsibility |
|:--|:--|
| **Cartographer** | Draws and maintains the party's map during exploration. Marks locations, routes, and points of interest as they are discovered. |
| **Lorekeeper** | Takes notes during play. Opens each new session with a short recap of the previous one. The Guide should not move on until the Lorekeeper has finished. |
| **Quartermaster** | Tracks shared party gold, supplies, and loot. Calls out when the party is running low on something important. |
| **Timekeeper** | Monitors the expedition clock. Reminds the party and Guide when time pressure exists, and tracks how much of the expedition window has been used. |
| **Leader** | Makes final calls when the party is split on a decision. Voices the party's official statements to NPCs when the group needs to speak as one. |

**Why this works:** Each role gives a player something to *do* that isn't their turn. The Cartographer is engaged during exploration. The Timekeeper is engaged during every scene. The Lorekeeper is invested between sessions. Players who feel responsible for something pay closer attention to it.

The Guide should actively prompt role players: *"Cartographer, we've just entered a new room — do you want to update the map?"* or *"Timekeeper, how are we looking on the clock?"*
