## [Running the Game](#running-the-game)

**Announce Target Numbers before the roll.**
Tell players the TN before they commit. *"That lock looks complex — TN15."* It speeds the table, makes decisions feel earned, and removes the feeling of rolling blind.

**Create roll opportunities constantly.**
The system rewards failure with XP. No roll means no XP chance. Actively build moments for skill checks — even when success is likely — so players are always progressing.

**Say yes to creative approaches.**
If a player uses a skill in an unexpected way, reward it with a lower TN rather than a refusal. A player who uses Persuade to calm a panicked horse deserves a roll.

**One NPC talks at a time.**
In scenes with multiple NPCs, only one of them speaks. Keeps social encounters from becoming a casting call. The others can react, but the main voice drives the scene.

**Telegraph enemy intent.**
Describe what enemies are *about to do* before they do it. *"The goblin draws back its arm — it's going to throw."* Keeps players engaged on turns that aren't theirs.

**Keep the clock visible.**
When a time pressure exists, remind players of it every few minutes at the table. Not as a threat — as information. Players make better decisions when they know the stakes are moving.
