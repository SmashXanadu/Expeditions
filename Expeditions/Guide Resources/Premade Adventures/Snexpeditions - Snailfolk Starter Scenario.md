
## Ideas
- Snoldier, Snorceror, Snecromancer.

## Background
- Snailfolk are an insular society that live almost exclusively in the Kingdom of Gastropa in the depths of the Uncharted Waters campaign world.
- Snailfolk deeply believe in many legends, mythcs, prophecies, etc.
- One of the Snailfolk ventured into the open air on a mission to obtain a powerful artifact to help their people. They never came back.
- This artifact was said to be have the ability to magically enable snailfolk to survive dry air indefinitely.

## Snailfolk
- **Aquatic**
	- You can breath and thrive underwater indefinitely.
	- When in water you can change your buoyancy to sink or float on demand.
	- If you are out of water for 45 minutes of real time you enter a state of forced hibernation. If you are not returned to the water within the next 15 minutes, you dry up and die.
- **Snail Sense:** You have poor eyesight and must rely more heavily on your improved senses of touch and smell.
  You have Disadvantage on rolls relying on vision, but Advantage on rolls relying on smell, taste, or touch.
- **Natural Armor**: On your turn you may choose to use your Action to withdraw into your shell to gain Advantage on all Evade and Resist rolls until your next turn.
- **Tentacles**: Your tentacles can reach out and manipulate small nearby objects.
- **Resistance to Cold**: You have a natural resistance to short periods of cold temperatures. If you are exposed to cold temperatures for an extended amount of time, you enter a state of forced hibernation. If you are not returned to warmer conditions within the next session, you dry up and die.
- **Weakness to Acid**: Your shell is particularly susceptible to Acid damage and must be avoided at all costs.
- **Slime Trail**: You leave a visible slime trail wherever you travel. Others can track you easily. Once per Expedition, you may coat a surface forcing any creature crossing it to Evade 12 or fall Prone.
- **Shell Storage**: Your shell can store up to 3 small items. They cannot be taken from you unless your shell is thoroughly damaged or broken.

---

- **Diet**: Herbivore that thrives on algae, plants, and natural sources of calcium such as bone to strengthen your shell.
- **Reproduction**: You are hermaphroditic and may reproduce with any other snailfolk, or reproduce asexually on your own.

## Scenario Rules
- 45-60 Minute timer on forced hibernation/drying out

## Story Beats
- Players leave the kingdom's water for the first time. Start the real-time dry-out clock visibly. Narrate the disorienting sensation of air, light, texture.
- Brief social/exploration beat — find tracks, a discarded item, or a surface creature that can point them in a direction. Introduce that the surface world doesn't know or care about snailfolk.
- A partially-wet environment (tidal flats, flooded ruins, a leaking dock). Gives the players some comfort before things get harder. Minor encounter here — something that preys on small creatures near the shore. Good place to introduce Snail Sense (smell/touch clues) vs. the disadvantage of poor vision. A hazard that a Slime Trail could solve, or that Shell Withdraw shines in.
- The trail leads into genuinely dry territory. The timer is now actively threatening. A mid-point encounter or obstacle — whoever has the envoy (or whatever blocked them) is here. Could be a combat, a social encounter, or a puzzle. Acid as an environmental hazard fits here (the weakness becomes terrifying).
- Find the envoy and/or the artifact. Options:
  - Alive but trapped: They found the artifact but are imprisoned. Rescue + escape.
  - Corrupted: The artifact changed them. They're now a boss or a tragic figure.
  - Dead, but they left the artifact behind: Emotional beat, clean resolution.
- The Race Home. The dry-out clock is dangerously low. Players race back to water, possibly pursued, possibly carrying an injured NPC or fragile artifact. A final hazard or chase rather than another combat — rewards clever use of Slime Trail, Tentacle reach, Shell Storage (protecting the artifact).
